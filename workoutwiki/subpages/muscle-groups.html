Lower

The lower body consists of everything from the hips down to the toes. Working on the lower body has numerous benefits to the overall health. You could become a much more efficient runner or swimmer, and your balance is improved greatly. The muscles which this area covers include but are not limited to the quads, glutes, and the calves. Often times when people report feeling back pain what they are really feeling is a tension coming from their legs, which connects to the back muscles in a way that if they are tight, they can also cause the back to be tight.

Core

The core is possible the most important area of the body to workout, and unfortunately this may get overlooked by many people. Its a very easy area to workout as well as you can very simply slip it in to the end of your workouts and reap huge benefits. The core helps significantly with balance and body control, and as the connection between your upper and lower extremities has a very versatile and broad area of effect. For example, pullups benefit from a tight core which keeps you from swinging, and squat lifts require a straight back and core in order to become effective and to be an overall healthy exercise!

Upper

The upper extremities or "upper body" consist of all the muscles from the chest to the neck, this includes the biceps, triceps, pectorals, deltoids, trapezius, etc. This is a very significant portion of the body to exercise because it governs the strength in some of the most basic everyday tasks we take upon ourselves, such as lifting heavy items. Working out the upper body makes you feel and appear to others as a more physically capable human being, and further, is the area which is the most aesthetically pleasing according to modern culture's view on fitness.
